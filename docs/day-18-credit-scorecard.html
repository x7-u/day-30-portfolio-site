About the project

Score a counterparty on the standard 5-factor model (liquidity, leverage, profitability, scale, payment behaviour) and draft the credit memo.

Local Flask app + CLI that grades a corporate credit file as LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH risk using a weighted multi-factor scorecard, then drafts the one-page credit memo a credit analyst would write before a lending decision.

At a glance

Framework
Weighted multi-factor scorecard, 14 features across 5 categories
Tiers
LOW < 30 / MEDIUM 30-60 / HIGH > 60
Inputs
Profitability + liquidity + leverage + efficiency + payment-history ratios
Outputs
Total score + tier + sub-scores + per-feature contributions + AI credit memo
Model
DeepSeek V4, single call (~$0.0005 per memo)

How it works

  • Score schema. score_schema.py wraps the inputs in a typed CreditInput dataclass.
  • Scoring engine. score_maths.py defines 14 features across 5 categories: profitability (gross / operating / net margin, ROA, ROE), liquidity (current + quick), leverage (debt-to-equity, interest...
  • AI credit memo. credit_memo.py builds a pre-summarised digest (headline score, tier, five category sub-scores, and the per-feature contribution table sorted by impact) and asks DeepSeek V4 for...
  • Institutional credit-research UI. Distinct from every prior day's palette.
  • Excel + CSV exports. openpyxl writes a 4-sheet workbook (Memo styled as a one-page credit note + Summary with embedded charts + Contributions + Inputs).

AI integration

DeepSeek drafts the credit memo

Limitations

  • Point-in-time only. Single-period view; no through-the-cycle scoring or PD curve.
  • No external data. All inputs come from the user; no integration with Companies House, credit bureaus, or KYC feeds.
  • Benchmark bands are textbook, not sector-calibrated.
  • No Altman Z comparison. Brief mentions Altman Z-Score side-by-side as post-30.
  • Recommendation thresholds are heuristic. LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH cutoffs at 30 and 60 are reasonable defaults; a real lender would calibrate to its own loss data.
  • AI proposes, does not opine. Every credit decision still needs a human credit officer's sign-off.

Fun facts

  • It scores a customer or supplier on the classic five factors of credit risk, then drafts the credit memo to go with the score.
  • The decision and the write-up, in one pass.
