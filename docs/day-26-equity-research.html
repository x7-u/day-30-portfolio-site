About the project

Drop in five years of financials; AI writes the rating, target price, investment thesis, bull and bear cases, risks and catalysts.

A small Flask app and CLI that takes a stock ticker plus a financial history and produces a one-page sell-side equity research note. The engine computes the multiples and the growth and margin signals; DeepSeek V4 writes the rating, target, thesis, bull and bear cases, and the risks and catalysts list.

At a glance

Use case
Sell-side equity research initiation notes
Inputs
5 years of financials per stock + sector + market data + consensus target
Outputs
Research note + financial-history XLSX + 3 CSVs + 3 charts
Model
Single DeepSeek call (~$0.0005 per note)

How it works

  • Schema. research_schema.py validates ticker (uppercased), sector from the 12 standard GICS-style sectors, currency in GBP / USD / EUR, market price and target price as non-negative...
  • Multiples engine. research_maths.compute walks the history year by year computing growth rates and margins.
  • AI research note. research_ai.draft builds a pipe-delimited digest of the history, the derived signals, the multiples, and the consensus comparison.
  • Sell-side research-note UI. Distinct from every prior day's palette.
  • Excel + CSV exports. openpyxl writes the multi-sheet workbook (Summary with charts embedded, Research note typeset, Financials grid year-by-year, Multiples, Inputs).

AI integration

DeepSeek drafts the research note

Limitations

  • Single-stock; no peer comparison. A real research note triangulates against 3 to 5 peers.
  • Trailing multiples only. Forward P/E and EV/EBITDA need consensus estimates.
  • No DCF on this day. Day 9 is the DCF model.
  • Output needs human review. AI-drafted notes are a starting point, not a publication-ready research note.
  • For educational use only. Not investment advice.

Fun facts

  • It writes a one-page sell-side research note (rating, target price, the bull case and the bear case) from five years of financials.
  • The sort of note that takes an analyst a day, drafted in a moment.
