About the project

Describe a business in plain English; AI designs the chart of accounts for entity type, industry, size and standard. Validated against the 1000-9999 convention.

A small Flask app and CLI that takes a plain-English business description and generates a complete, structured chart of accounts appropriate for the entity type, industry, size and accounting standard. DeepSeek V4 designs the COA; a rules-based validator confirms uniqueness, code ranges and normal balances.

At a glance

Use case
Chart of accounts design for new ledgers, accounting system setup
Inputs
Plain-English business description + entity type + industry + size + accounting standard
Outputs
Validated COA + 3 charts + Excel workbook + 3 CSVs
Model
Single DeepSeek call (~$0.0008 per generation)

How it works

  • Schema. coa_schema.py validates entity type from a 7-option enum, industry from 14 sectors, size band from {micro, small, medium, large}, accounting standard from {FRS 102, FRS 105, IFRS,...
  • Validation engine. coa_maths.validate enforces the UK 1000-9999 convention: 1000s assets, 2000s liabilities, 3000s equity, 4000s revenue, 5000-9999 expenses.
  • AI design. coa_ai.draft sends the business context to DeepSeek V4 with a structured JSON schema requesting 25-55 accounts (sized by size_band) across all five sections, with...
  • Bookkeeping-ledger UI. Distinct from every prior day's palette.
  • Excel + CSV exports. openpyxl writes the multi-sheet workbook (Summary with embedded charts, Accounts sheet with section-coloured rows, Validation, Rationale, Inputs).

AI integration

DeepSeek designs the chart of accounts

Limitations

  • UK 1000-9999 convention only. US firms often use 10000-99999 (five digit).
  • Output needs human review. Generated COAs need review by a qualified accountant before being loaded into a production accounting system.
  • No accounting-system integration. Export to Xero, QuickBooks, Sage import format is post-30.
  • Generic UK GAAP / IFRS overlay. Specialist industries (insurance, banking, oil and gas, agriculture) need their own overlay templates.
  • Hard-coded to the 8 most common industries. Other industries get the generic overlay only.

Fun facts

  • Describe a business in a sentence and it designs the whole chart of accounts for it, sized and structured for the industry.
  • It keeps to the standard 1000 to 9999 numbering convention without being told.
