About the project

Economic order quantity, reorder point, and safety stock for inventory items with AI recommendations.

Local-only Flask web app and CLI that takes SKU-level sales + stock data, computes the textbook turnover metrics (inventory turnover ratio, days sales of inventory, ABC Pareto class, velocity class), flags slow-moving / dead / excess stock, computes EOQ + reorder point + safety stock per SKU, and asks DeepSeek V4 for per-SKU action recommendations framed in working-capital terms.

At a glance

Inputs
3 CSVs (SKU master + 6-month sales history + current stock)
Outputs
Per-SKU turnover + DSI + ABC + velocity, EOQ + safety stock + reorder point, AI per-SKU action plan in working-capital terms
Model
DeepSeek V4 (deepseek-chat), one call per analysis
Cost
around $0.001 per analysis

How it works

  • Inventory schema. inventory_schema.py parses three CSVs: SKU master (sku_id, name, category, unit_cost, lead_time_days, supplier), 6-month sales history (date, sku_id, units_sold, revenue), and...
  • Inventory metrics. inventory_metrics.py computes per SKU: total units sold, revenue, COGS, avg daily units, on-hand, inventory value, turnover ratio (units_sold / on_hand), DSI (365 / turnover),...
  • EOQ + reorder point. eoq_calculator.py computes per SKU: annual demand (extrapolated from the period), EOQ via the Wilson formula (sqrt(2 * D * S / H), with S = order_cost and H = unit_cost *...
  • AI recommender. ai_recommender.py builds a digest of the worst performers + EOQ gaps + headline stats (~600 input tokens) and asks DeepSeek V4 in JSON mode for: summary, per-SKU diagnosis +...
  • Warehouse-tag UI. Distinct from Day 7 (Bloomberg), Day 8 (glass orbs), Day 9 (parchment atelier), Day 10 (magazine bold blocks), Day 11 (FT pink broadsheet), Day 12 (matte engineering drawing), Day...

AI integration

DeepSeek recommends stocking levels

Limitations

  • Single period only. One 6-month window; comparing two periods is post-30.
  • ABC on revenue, not gross margin. Margin-weighted ABC is a small change.
  • Constant lead time per SKU. Lead-time variability not modelled; tightens safety stock when added.
  • EOQ assumes constant demand + instantaneous replenishment.
  • Single location. Multi-warehouse netting + redistribution is post-30.
  • No demand forecasting. Brief lists ARIMA / Prophet as post-30; we agree.

Fun facts

  • It works out exactly how much stock to order and when, balancing the cost of holding inventory against the cost of running out.
  • Economic order quantity, the classic textbook formula, brought to life with real recommendations.
