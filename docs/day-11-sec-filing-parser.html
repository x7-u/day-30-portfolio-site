About the project

Parse SEC 10-K filings from EDGAR; extract the risk section, executive summary, and key financials.

Local-only Flask web app and CLI that pulls a 10-K (or 10-Q) filing from SEC EDGAR for any US-listed company, parses five years of XBRL financials into structured statements + ratios, extracts the Risk Factors section from the 10-K HTML, and asks DeepSeek V4 to rank the five most material and most specific risks. Each AI flag carries a verbatim citation back to the filing source text.

At a glance

DeepSeek V4 (deepseek-chat), one call per filing (deterministic fallback if offline)
Cost
around $0.001 per filing on the AAPL sample, well under the 1c ceiling

How it works

  • EDGAR client. edgar.py wraps SEC's free public API. Requires a real User-Agent header (we ship a generic one and let the user override via DAY11_EDGAR_UA).
  • XBRL parser. xbrl_parser.py walks the companyfacts JSON and collapses the multi-decade dataset to the most recent five fiscal years of the four canonical statements.
  • 10-K HTML parser. filing_parser.py uses BeautifulSoup with regex anchors to locate Item 1A.
  • AI risk extractor. risk_extractor.py sends only the lead sentences to DeepSeek V4 (around 3-5k tokens) rather than the full 50k-word section.
  • Newsprint UI. Distinct from Day 7 (Bloomberg terminal black), Day 8 (glass orbs gradient), Day 9 (parchment + navy serif italic + gold), Day 10 (sage cream + bold colour blocks + heavy...
  • Excel and CSV exports. openpyxl writes a 7-sheet workbook (Summary + Income + Balance + Cash Flow + Ratios + Risk Factors + Risk Verbatim).
