About the project

Bucket-by-bucket ageing (0-30, 30-60, 60-90, 90+) across suppliers, with AI flagging unusual delays.

Local-only Flask web app and CLI for accounts payable ageing analysis. Loads invoice data into an in-memory SQLite database, runs the aged trial balance in SQL, computes deterministic risk signals per supplier, and asks DeepSeek V4 to rank the highest-risk suppliers and recommend an action for each.

At a glance

Model
DeepSeek V4 (deepseek-chat), one call per analysis (rule-based fallback if offline)
Cost
around $0.001 per analysis, $0 with --skip-ai

How it works

  • CSV parser. ap_schema.parse_csv accepts column aliases (Sage, Xero, NetSuite, in-house exports) and walks utf-8-sig / utf-8 / cp1252 / latin-1 encodings.
  • SQLite layer. Brief calls for 'Python + SQL' so we use sqlite3 from stdlib.
  • Severity classifier. Each supplier lands in low / medium / high / critical.
  • AI flagger. Single DeepSeek V4 call per analysis.
  • Magazine editorial aesthetic. Distinct from Day 7 (Bloomberg terminal black), Day 8 (glass orbs gradient) and Day 9 (parchment + navy serif).
  • Excel and CSV exports. openpyxl writes a 6-sheet workbook (Summary + Supplier Ageing + Invoices + Risk Flags + Cash Required + SQL).

AI integration

DeepSeek flags unusual ageing patterns

Fun facts

  • It buckets every supplier invoice by how overdue it is (0-30, 30-60, 60-90, and 90 plus days) and points at the delays that look unusual.
  • The kind of report a treasury team lives by, produced in seconds.
